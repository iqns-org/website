Compliance is brittle when different teams interpret the same requirement differently.

## Why this matters

When regulators and teams speak the same semantic language, audit friction disappears.

## What this looks like in practice

- A compliance requirement reads the same whether in policies or encoded in software controls.
- Audit trails answer the same questions pulled from logs, human processes, or AI systems.
- Risk assessments use identical criteria across frameworks, regions, and business units.

## How teams use it

- connecting regulatory language to control implementation without manual translation
- tracking compliance artifacts across audit, operations, and risk with shared definitions
- proving equivalence between legacy controls and new technology implementations

Teams passing audits fastest have consistent definitions, not just more controls.
